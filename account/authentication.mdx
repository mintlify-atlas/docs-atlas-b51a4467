Trabajonautas supports two ways to sign in: email and password, or your Google account via OAuth. Both methods give you access to the same dashboard and features. A few security measures — including email verification, single-session enforcement, and optional two-factor authentication — help keep your account safe.

## Signing in

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Email and password">
    ### Registering with email

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Create your account">
        Go to the registration page and fill in your name, email address, and a password. You must accept the terms and conditions to proceed.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify your email">
        After registering, Trabajonautas sends a verification email to the address you provided. Click the link in that email to confirm your address. You cannot access the dashboard until your email is verified.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Complete your profile">
        Once verified, log in and finish setting up your profile — including your location, profession, and other details.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    ### Logging in with email

    Go to the login page and enter your email and password. For privacy, the login form displays your email in an obfuscated format (e.g. `jua***@gmail.com`) once it is recognized — only the first three characters of the local part are shown.

    ### Resetting your password

    If you forget your password, use the password reset link on the login page. You'll receive a reset email with a secure link to set a new password.

    <Note>
      Password reset emails are sent from a custom Trabajonautas notification. Check your spam folder if you don't see the email within a few minutes.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Google OAuth">
    ### Signing in with Google

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Click 'Sign in with Google'">
        On the login page, click the Google sign-in button to initiate the OAuth flow.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Authorize with your Google account">
        Select your Google account and grant Trabajonautas permission to access your name and email address.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Account created or linked automatically">
        If no Trabajonautas account exists for your Google email, a new account is created automatically with your Google name and email. Your email is marked as verified immediately — no separate email confirmation step is required.
      </Step>
      <Step title="You're signed in">
        You are logged in and redirected to your dashboard.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Tip>
      If you previously registered with the same email address using a password, signing in with Google will log you into that existing account. Your accounts are matched by email address.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Email verification

Trabajonautas requires that your email address be verified before you can access the authenticated areas of the platform. This applies to email/password registrations. Google OAuth sign-ins are automatically verified.

If your email is not verified, you will be blocked from accessing your dashboard and other protected routes until you click the verification link sent to your inbox.

## Single-session enforcement

Trabajonautas enforces **one active session per account**. When you log in on a new device or browser, all your other active sessions are terminated automatically.

This means:

- If you are logged in on your phone and then log in on a laptop, your phone session ends.
- If someone else logs into your account, your current session will be invalidated.

<Warning>
  If you are unexpectedly logged out, it may mean that someone else has signed into your account. Change your password immediately and enable two-factor authentication.
</Warning>

## Two-factor authentication (2FA)

Trabajonautas supports two-factor authentication via a time-based authenticator app (TOTP). When enabled, you must enter a 6-digit code from your authenticator app each time you log in.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to your profile settings">
    Open your account settings from the dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable two-factor authentication">
    Find the two-factor authentication section and click **Enable**. A QR code will be displayed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scan the QR code">
    Open your authenticator app (such as Google Authenticator or Authy) and scan the QR code to add Trabajonautas.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your recovery codes">
    You will be shown a set of one-time recovery codes. Store these in a safe place. They are the only way to regain access if you lose your authenticator device.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and activate">
    Enter a code from your authenticator app to confirm setup. 2FA is now active on your account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Enabling 2FA is strongly recommended, especially if you use the platform to manage job postings or company information. It adds a second layer of protection beyond your password.
</Tip>

## Account activation and deactivation

Every Trabajonautas account has an `actived` status. Accounts that are deactivated by an administrator cannot log in and are shown a deactivation notice. If you believe your account has been deactivated in error, contact Trabajonautas support.

## Security tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use a strong, unique password">
    Choose a password that you don't use on any other site. A mix of letters, numbers, and symbols makes your account significantly harder to compromise.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Enable two-factor authentication">
    2FA is the single most effective step you can take to protect your account. Even if your password is exposed, 2FA prevents unauthorized access.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Understand single-session behavior">
    Because Trabajonautas allows only one active session at a time, always log out from shared or public devices. Being logged out unexpectedly is a signal that your credentials may have been used elsewhere.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Keep your recovery codes safe">
    If you enable 2FA, your recovery codes are the only way back in if you lose access to your authenticator app. Store them offline in a secure location.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use Google Sign-In for a passwordless option">
    Signing in with Google removes the risk of a weak or reused password. Google OAuth sessions still follow Trabajonautas's single-session enforcement rules.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
